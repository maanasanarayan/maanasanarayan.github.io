  This is the third in a series of "**one dot**" mugs I've been working
  on since 2023. The constraint is the point: one mark, made with one
  brush, in under three seconds, after the mug is otherwise finished.
  You can't fix it. You can't add another. You either commit to the
  placement or you start over. It's the most useful drawing practice
  I've found.

Thrown on the wheel, trimmed at leather-hard, bisque-fired, then
underglazed and clear-glazed. The dot was a single sable-7 brush
loaded once.
